# The Signal Method

**A framework by Dreamfree for cutting through marketing noise and amplifying the signals that make your ideal customers take action.**

---

## The Core Idea

Every business gives off signals—through its words, its design, its presence, and its behaviour. Most businesses give off the *wrong* signals: muddled messaging, generic design, no measurement, no adaptation. The result is noise, not communication.

The Signal Method is a curated synthesis of proven marketing and communication principles—drawn from frameworks like StoryBrand, They Ask You Answer, and Sales Safari—combined with practical experience and shaped into a single, coherent system.

It exists to answer one question: **Is your marketing giving off the right signals to the right people at the right time?**

---

## The Unifying Principle: Coherence

Across every pillar of the Signal Method, one principle holds everything together: **coherence**. Every signal a business sends—across every channel, every page, every interaction—must tell a consistent story. Incoherence doesn't just dilute results; it actively prevents them.

---

## The Seven Pillars

### 1. Research — Know What They Actually Want

Before you communicate anything, you need to understand the real needs, desires, and frustrations of your customers. Not what you *think* they want. What they actually say, feel, and search for.

This means going to where your customers already congregate—forums, social media, reviews, conversations—and listening to their language, their pain, and their questions. The goal is ethnographic understanding, not assumptions.

*Draws from: Sales Safari (Amy Hoy), They Ask You Answer (Marcus Sheridan)*

---

### 2. Messaging — Tell the Right Story

Your copy and content must be built on story—not features, not jargon, not cleverness. Story creates emotional resonance and positions the customer as the hero of their own journey.

This pillar also covers the creation of evergreen content that answers the real questions customers ask: pricing, comparisons, problems, and best-of guides. Content that builds trust by being genuinely useful rather than promotional.

*Draws from: StoryBrand (Donald Miller), They Ask You Answer (Marcus Sheridan)*

---

### 3. Design — Support the Signal, Don't Compete With It

Design exists to serve the business aim, not to impress other designers. The visual presentation of a website, a brand, or a piece of content should amplify the message—not distract from it.

This means professional, intentional design where every element earns its place. It's the opposite of drag-and-drop templates that look polished but achieve nothing.

---

### 4. Credibility — Build the Proof

Signals only land if the audience trusts the sender. Credibility is built deliberately through case studies, testimonials, social proof, transparency, and demonstrated expertise.

This isn't a prerequisite that exists before the method—it's an active, ongoing output of it. Every piece of content, every result shared, every honest answer to a hard question builds the trust bank.

---

### 5. Measurement — Know What's Working

Without measurement, marketing is guesswork. The Signal Method insists on defining what success actually looks like for *this* business—not applying a template metric sheet.

Not every business is optimising for sales. Some want enquiries, some want awareness, some want community. The measurement framework must reflect the real business objective, then track performance against it.

---

### 6. Experimentation — Stay Willing to Be Wrong

No framework survives contact with reality unchanged. The Signal Method builds in a bias toward testing, learning, and adjusting.

This means A/B testing, exploring how new technology (especially AI) impacts marketing, challenging assumptions, and never being dogmatic about what works. The market moves. The method moves with it.

---

### 7. Personalisation — Make It About Them

People pay attention to things that are about *them*. Personalisation—powered increasingly by AI—allows businesses to deliver tailored experiences at scale.

This doesn't mean abandoning the framework. The skeleton stays the same; the examples, applications, and context shift to match the individual. Same course modules, but with custom sections drawn from the client's own data and situation.

## What the Signal Method Is Not

- It is not a single marketing tactic.
- It is not a rigid, one-size-fits-all playbook.
- It is not an invention from scratch—it's a disciplined curation of what works, informed by real experience.
- It is not about making fancy websites. It's about making websites that *work*.

## Origin

The Signal Method was developed by Daniel Whittaker at Dreamfree, drawing on years of experience in web design, digital marketing, and client strategy. It synthesises proven frameworks with a practitioner's instinct for what actually moves the needle—and discards what doesn't.
